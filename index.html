About Laguna

Laguna is a province in the Philippines' Calabarzon region on the island of Luzon. It is one of the Philippines' most developed, historical, artistic and cultural provinces in the Philippines. Laguna has 7 cities and 24 municiaplities and is divided into three (3) major region.

Cities

Because of the rapid suburbanization brought about by Manila's outer expansion, most cities in Laguna have become essentially residential towns, and much of their history and culture has been lost to oblivion.

Weather

Except for the northwestern part, where there are dry and wet seasons, the area climate is characterized by regular rain showers throughout the year. When visiting most of Laguna, it is vital to keep in mind the frequent fluctuations in weather.

Language

Tagalog is the major language. The Tagalog dialect in Laguna is similar to that of Manila, but with vocabulary influences from Batangas and Quezon dialects.

Government Officials

The seat of the provincial government of Laguna is Santa Cruz, and has been the capital since 1858. Historically, the provincial government was seated in Bay (1581-1688) and Pagsanjan (1688-1858) before setting in Santa Cruz. The provincial government is organized under a governor and vice governor, as well as the Laguna Provincial Board.
