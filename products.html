Local Foods

The Philippines stands out among its neighboring countries in terms of its unique food recognition, particularly when it comes to delicacies. Filipinos have a tradition of purchasing famous products whenever they visit a specific area. Every region in the Philippines has its own distinct sweet delicacies that they take pride in, such as the Province of Laguna.


Laguna is known for its diverse and delicious food products. The region has a rich culinary heritage and is renowned for its traditional delicacies such as pancit habhab, buko pie, and kakanin. In addition to these local favorites, Laguna is also famous for its distinctive and unique food products. These products are typically made from locally-sourced ingredients and are grown in different municipalities throughout the province. Some examples of these products include the sweet and savory longganisa (sausage) from Los Banos, the delectable lechon (roasted pig) from Pagsanjan, and the mouth-watering puto bumbong (purple rice cake) from Pila. These delicacies are not only a treat for the taste buds, but also a reflection of the province's rich cultural heritage.

Laguna, a province located in the Philippines, is known for its delicious local delicacies, particularly kakanin. Kakanin is a term used to describe a variety of traditional Filipino desserts and snacks made from glutinous rice or other starchy ingredients such as sweet potato, yam, and cassava. Some popular kakanin from Laguna include suman, bibingka, and kalamay. These treats are often enjoyed during special occasions and are available in many local markets and street vendors in the province.



Local Products

Whittling or Woodshaving Products of Pakil, Laguna

Pahiyas Tambag, also known as Woodshaving or Wood Whittling, is a traditional and cultural wedding banquet that is decorated with Spanish influence dating back to the 18th century. Unfortunately, it is becoming a dying art in the Philippines, with only a few practitioners remaining in the area of Pakil, Laguna.

Wooden Bags and Wood and Metal Sculpture of Paete, Laguna

Paete, officially recognized as the Carving Capital of the Philippines by Presidential Proclamation in 2005, has a long history of skilled wood carvers, dating back even before the Spanish colonial period. The town is known for its artistic craftsmanship, with many traditional handmade items created using local materials.

The Embroidery Captial of the Philippines - Lumban

Lumban, Laguna, is known as the Embroidery Capital of the Philippines. It is a prime destination for those interested in embroidery and weaving. The town offers a wide range of products, from traditional Barong Tagalogs, Saya, and Kimonos to more modern fully-calladoed gowns. The intricate embroidery on each piece showcases not only passion but also artistry passed down through generations.

Water Hyacinth Products of Pila, Laguna

Pila, Laguna, is known for its many ancestral houses and historical structures, but it also has a hidden gem in its craftsmanship, particularly in the production of products made from water hyacinth, such as RolyoLikha and Sarilikha. Water hyacinth is difficult to remove due to its high rate of regeneration. However, the people of Pila have found a way to make use of the plant by using its fibrous stem to create various products such as coasters, slippers, baskets, hats, fruit trays, furniture, handbags, and women’s purses.

Laguna is known for its wide range of products that are made locally, such as textiles, ceramics, and food items. Some popular products include the traditional "banig" (mat), "barong Tagalog" (a formal men's shirt), and "patadyong" (a type of cloth). The region is also known for its ceramics, particularly the "Paete" and "Liliw" pottery. Additionally, there are many food products that are produced in Laguna, such as "kakanin" (rice cakes), "puto" (steamed rice cakes), and "leche flan" (custard). Many of these products are not only popular in the Philippines but also internationally, making them great souvenirs to bring back home.
