About Laguna

Province in the Philippines

About Laguna

Laguna is a province in the Philippines' Calabarzon region on the island of Luzon. It is one of the Philippines' most developed, historical, artistic and cultural provinces in the Philippines. Laguna has 7 cities and 24 municiaplities and is divided into three (3) major region.

AREA

Laguna is a province in the Philippines. It is composed of three major regions, which are known for their distinct characteristics. These regions are connected by a main hub, which allows for easy access to the different sites within each region. Additionally, each region has its own unique topography, which can include features such as mountains, hills, valleys, and coastal areas. The regions are:

Eastern Laguna

Rainy region at the western side of Sierra Madre; major tourist draws are the Pagsanjan Falls, Lake Caliraya, and the wood sculptures of Paete. It is a region that is characterized by rolling hills and flatlands. It is home to many of Laguna's major cities and towns, including the capital city of Santa Cruz, as well as important economic and industrial centers.

Northwest Laguna

A combination of modern and densely populated neighborhoods characterized by urban expansion, with popular attractions such as hot springs, a historic mansion, an amusement park, and charming downtown areas featuring Spanish colonial-style churches. Including Jose Rizal's Mansion, the famous Enchanted Kingdom and many more.

Southern Laguna

A region with dense forest and mountainous terrain, consisting of various small towns and the main city of San Pablo, known for its Seven Lakes, an underground cemetery, and traditional wooden footwear made in the town of Liliw.


All these regions have different culture, dialect and also different way of livelihood. Thus each of them have their own unique feature. Different regions have a distinct culture and way of livelihood. These factors shape the unique features that make each region distinct and different from one another.


CITIES

Because of the rapid suburbanization brought about by Manila's outer expansion, most cities in Laguna have become essentially residential towns, and much of their history and culture has been lost to oblivion. Laguna has 7 cities and these are:

1. Santa Cruz

Provincial Capital

2. Biñan

Now a large suburb of Manila, it was where Jose Rizal have his first education

3. Calamba

Hometown of Jose Rizal and a city of hot springs

4. Cabuyao

An out-of-the-way residential and industrial city, with some notable churches

5. San Pablo

Largest city in Laguna in terms of land mass. The "City of Seven Lakes".

6. San Pedro

A growing residential and industrial suburb not far from Metro Manila. Home of the Sampaguita Festival and the Shrine of the Holy Sepulchre.

7. Santa Rosa

A growing residential suburb and main gateway to Tagaytay, with an amusement park, malls,.

LANGUAGE

Tagalog is the major language. The Tagalog dialect in Laguna is similar to that of Manila, but with vocabulary influences from Batangas and Quezon dialects. // The majority of Laguna inhabitants, particularly those living in metropolitan areas, can communicate in English. In terms of dialect, regions can also have different variations of a language. This can be influenced by factors such as location, history, and migration patterns. Additionally, some regions may have their own unique dialects or languages that are not spoken in other parts of the country.


OTHER DETAILS

Laguna also has its share of its historical contributions, such as the Laguna Copperplate Inscription dated back to 700 CE and the birthplace of Philippines' national hero, Jose Rizal. Most of the settlements in Laguna dates back to the Spanish era, and provides a glance on its colonial past.

Laguna is also another frequented tourist destination not far from Metro Manila. Los Baños is abundant on hot spring resorts, and Calamba is another important location as Jose Rizal's hometown and also has its share of resorts. Pagsanjan, just east of the provincial capital in Santa Cruz, is home to the frequented Pagsanjan Falls.

Except for the northwestern part, where there are dry and wet seasons, the area climate is characterized by regular rain showers throughout the year. When visiting most of Laguna, it is vital to keep in mind the frequent fluctuations in weather.
