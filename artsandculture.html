Arts & Culture

Province of Laguna

Arts & Culture

Laguna plays a significant role in promoting arts in the Philippines. Paete, in particular, is renowned as the Woodcarving Capital of the Philippines due to the intricate murals found in the Saint James the Apostle Parish Church

Like the rest of the Philippines, Laguna celebrates various feasts and festivals throughout the year, with each barangay having their own fiesta honoring a patron saint.

Old Churches

Laguna has a rich history of religious architecture, due in large part to Spanish influence. Many of these churches have been well-preserved to this day. Four of the most notable churches in the province are the Church of Santa Rosa de Lima, the San Policarpio Church, the San Antonio de Padua Church, and the Church of Saint John the Baptist. These four churches are popular destinations for the annual Visita Iglesia, a tradition that is practiced by many Filipinos nationwide and is typically done on Maundy Thursday.

Crafts of Paete

Paete, Laguna has a reputation for being home to skilled artisans who specialize in woodcarving - from large statues of saints to small sculptures and wall decor. The town is also recognized for its papier-mache horse crafts, known as "taka." With a history of artistry spanning over a hundred years, Paete was officially named the "Carving Capital of the Philippines" in 2005.

National Hero

The province of Laguna is home to many important figures in Philippine history, including the country's national hero, Dr. Jose P. Rizal. These individuals, along with many others, have played a significant role in shaping the province's rich history, culture, customs, and traditions. Many of these traditions are still practiced today by both older and younger generations of locals in Laguna.

    Famous Rizal sites in Laguna
  • Rizal Monument
  • Rizal Park and Shrine
  • Museo ni Jose Rizal (Bahay ni Rizal)
  • Paciano Rizal Shrine (Jose Rizal's brother)

Customs

In addition to historical sites, Laguna is also known for its unique customs and traditions that can only be found in certain areas of the province. For example, in Magdalena, Laguna, there is a wedding tradition called Galahan, in which newlywed couples are required to dance in front of their relatives. Another custom found in Laguna is Bayanihan, where neighbors help each other out in times of need. This hospitality and helpfulness is what makes tourists come back to the province again and again. Only in Laguna can one experience this kind of warm welcome.
