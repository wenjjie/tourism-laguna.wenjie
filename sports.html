PSGDO launches GRLH Serbisyong Tama Free Sports Clinics 2022

Provincial Games and Sports Development Office, June 15, 2022

On June 7th, the head of the Provincial Sports and Games Development Office, Mr. Mario Tobias, held a meeting with coaches as part of the 2022 Gov. Ramil L. Hernandez (GRLH) Serbisyong Tama Free Sports Clinics at the Laguna Sports Complex Basketball Gymnasium in Santa Cruz, Laguna.

The Provincial Sports and Games Development Office, led by Mr. Mario Tobias, organized a meeting for coaches on June 7 at the Laguna Sports Complex Basketball Gymnasium in Sta. Cruz, Laguna to discuss the Gov. Ramil L. Hernandez (GRLH) Serbisyong Tama Free Sports Clinics 2022. The PSGDO, in partnership with Local Sports Associations and coaches from Laguna, will be offering free clinics in 13 different sports disciplines to be held in various cities and municipalities throughout the province from June to August 2022. The program, which is part of the PSGDO's grassroots sports initiative, aims to identify and train local athletes for higher-level competitions such as the Philippine Sports Commission's Philippine Youth Games or Batang Pinoy.

PSGDO holds two Governor's Cup chess tournaments

Provincial sports and Games Development Office, March 29, 2022

The PSGDO, in collaboration with the LCA, held two Governor's Cup Tournaments, the 3rd Laguna Inter-Town Chess Team Championships and the 1st Laguna Chess King and Queen Challenge, at the Laguna Sports Complex in Bubukal, Sta. Cruz, Laguna on March 13 and 20, 2022 respectively.

The Provincial Sports and Games Development Office (PSGDO) in collaboration with the Laguna Chess Association (LCA) organized two Governor's Cup Tournaments in March 2022 at the Laguna Sports Complex in Bubukal, Santa Cruz, Laguna. The 3rd Laguna Inter-Town Chess Team Championships was held on March 13 and the 1st Laguna Chess King and Queen Challenge on March 20. PSGDO ICO, Mr. Mario Tobias, and LCA President, Mr. Richie Jocson, were pleased with the high turnout this year, which exceeded pre-pandemic numbers. A total of 65 teams (11 in the Junior Division and 54 in the Open Division) from different cities and municipalities participated in the team championships. Meanwhile, 134 participants joined the 1st Gov. Ramil L. Hernandez Laguna Chess King and Queen Challenge. The tournament was won by Calamba City A in the Open Division and Cabuyao Junior A in the Junior Division, while Michael Concio Jr. brought home the championship in the King and Queen Challenge. Cash prizes, trophies, medals and special awards were given to the top teams and players. Mr. Tobias is grateful for the support of Governor Hernandez, Provincial Administrator Atty. Dulce H. Rebanal, and the Provincial Government in the sports development efforts of PSGDO. He also plans to return other sports development programs in the province.

(Greyz Caballes, photo by PSGDO/PIO Laguna)
