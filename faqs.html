Laguna is a province situated in the Calabarzon region on the island of Luzon in the Philippines. Known for its rich history, culture and heritage, it is considered as one of the most progressive provinces in the country. The province boasts a wide range of attractions, from scenic lakes and waterfalls, to historical sites and cultural landmarks. Many of the towns and cities in Laguna are known for their cultural and historical significance, and are popular among both locals and tourists. Laguna is a province located in the CALABARZON region of the Philippines, occupying the central part of the island of Luzon. The municipality of Santa Cruz serves as its capital. The province has a land area of 1,928.23 square kilometers or 744.49 square miles and a population of 3,382,193, as recorded in the 2020 Census.

The Province of Laguna is situated in the CALABARZON region of the Philippines, occupying the central part of the island of Luzon. The province was named after Laguna de Bay, which is the body of water that forms its northern boundary. Laguna de Bay is the largest lake in the Philippines, and it is a significant source of fish and other aquatic resources for the local communities. The name of the lake, in turn, was derived from the town of Bay, which was the first provincial capital of Laguna. Bay was the first Spanish settlement in the province, and it was established in 1571. The name "Laguna de Bay" is Spanish for "Lake of Bay", reflecting the historical significance of the town in the naming of the province and the lake.
