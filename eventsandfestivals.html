Events and Festivals in Laguna

Cultural identity is a vital aspect of social empowerment, as it allows people to confidently nurture and develop their own unique characters, concepts, and ideas. The practice of distinct cultural skills also brings social and economic benefits, such as the enrichment of arts and crafts, culinary creativity, and colorful celebrations. A strong sense of community can also lead to a positive self-image and a foundation of unity that is tolerant of cultural diversity.



In order to tap into the greatest asset of the people for sustainable development, the Provincial Government of Laguna supports the holding of festivals to cultivate and harness cultural strengths and resources. This way, the government promotes people participation, local genius, and cultural identity. Because local communities, not the state, are the ultimate actors in the development process, provincial governance is most effective and productive when it is culturally rooted. Encouraging celebration of the unique identities of local communities through various activities and forms can inspire harmony and economic engagement in society.



In Laguna, all cities and municipalities have their own festivals that reflect the uniqueness of their communities.

Coconut Festival

San Pablo, Laguna

The Coconut Tree, also known as the "Tree of Life," is a valuable resource for human survival. Each of its parts can be used in different ways to support human life. The Coconut Festival, or "Coco Fest," is a week-long event held annually in January in San Pablo, Laguna, starting in 1996 to honor the patron saint, Saint Paul the Hermit and to acknowledge the importance of the coconut tree in Philippines, particularly in the City and Province of Laguna.

Anilag Festival

Sta. Cruz, Laguna

The Anilag Festival, also known as the "Mother of Harvest Festivals," is an annual weeklong event held in March in the capital town of Sta. Cruz in Laguna. Despite being first celebrated in 2004, it has quickly become one of the most significant festivals in the region. The festival focuses on celebrating the income generated by Laguna's successful agriculture and tourism industries, with a special emphasis on the unique delicacies that are produced in the area, such as Buko pie, puto, espasol, salted eggs, battered duck, bulalo, white cheese, and local macapuno bibingka.

Pinya Festival Calauan, Laguna

The Pinya Festival is celebrated annually on May 15th by the municipality of Calauan, Laguna in honor of their patron saint, St. Isidore Laborer, the patron of farmers. The festival, which began in 1999, aims to promote the town's pineapple and other agricultural products. The event features a trade fair show with a variety of pineapple-related products, such as delicacies and textiles made from pineapple leaves. Additionally, there is a parade with decorated floats and a street dancing competition with colorful costumes. Calauan is known for producing some of the sweetest pineapples in the CALABARZON region.

Tsinelas Festival

Liliw, Laguna

The Gat Tayaw Tsinelas Festival in Liliw, Philippines, is held annually during the last week of April and the first week of May in honor of the town's founder, Gat Tayaw. The festival showcases the agricultural and industrial products of the town, including its primary industry, the shoe and slipper industry, as well as highlighting historical and cultural themes. The festival is named after the sound of a bird's song, which inspired the name of the town.

Itik Festival

Victoria, Laguna

Victoria, known as the "Duck Raising Center of the Philippines," is a popular destination for traders of balut. To celebrate this and the town's founding anniversary, the Itik Festival is held annually on the second of November. The festival, which began in November 2002, attracts tourists and students from neighboring towns to observe the raising of itik and see products made from it. One of the main attractions of the festival is the street dance competition that features participants from both public and private schools. Visitors can also visit the famous Tindahan ng Itlog ni Kuya at Iba Pa, a shop located along the highway of Victoria, Laguna that offers Pekin Kinulob, Lechon Kinulob na Itik, and Itlog na Maalat (salted egg).

Puto Latik Festival

Biñan, Laguna

The Puto Latik Festival, which has been held annually during the first week of February since 2011, is a commemoration of the traditional rice cake delicacy known as Puto Biñan and the Maglalatik traditional dance from Biñan. However, starting in 2017, the festival will be celebrated during May 15th -23rd, to align with the historical significance of the Maglalatik in the celebration of the feast day of San Isidro Labrador, the city's patron saint.

Sampaguita Festival

San Pedro, Laguna

The City Festival is an annual event celebrated during the second week of February, featuring a variety of activities such as cultural performances, sports, trade fairs, singing contests, parades, historical exhibits, social and religious gatherings, tribal dances, street dances, cheering and sport exhibitions. The highlight of the festival is the coronation night of the "Hiyas ng San Pedro". The goal of the festival is to promote tourism in San Pedro and to revitalize the community's Sampaguita industry. The festival was originally known as the "Manok ni San Pedro Festival" starting in 1999, then it was renamed to "Sampaguita Festival" in 2002. It begins with a grand parade.

Paet Taka Festival

Paeta, Laguna

The town of Paete gets its name from the Tagalog word "paet," which means chisel. The correct way to pronounce the town's name is believed to be "Pa-e-te," but the locals pronounce it as "Pī-té," with a long "i" and short guttural "ê" sound at the end. The town's inhabitants primarily use the first pronunciation when speaking with outsiders, while the American Maryknoll Missioners referred to the town as "Piety" when they arrived in the late 1950s.

3 Kings Festivals

Mabitac, Laguna

The Feast of the Three Kings, also known as the Feast of the Epiphany, is a significant event among Filipino Catholics that is celebrated on January 6th. It is a time for feasting, visiting, and gift-giving and marks the end of the Christmas celebration in the Philippines before students return to school after their long Christmas vacation. In the Philippines, the celebration of Epiphany is also known as Pasko ng Matatanda, or the Feast of the Elderly, to honor senior citizens.
