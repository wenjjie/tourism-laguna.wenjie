Tourism

Province of Laguna

TOURISM


Laguna has a thriving tourism industry due to its rich history and natural attractions. The local government's tourism department has officially recognized various establishments in the province, including hotels, resorts, restaurants, tour operators, and museums. Additionally, Laguna has a significant number of approved tourist destinations, second only to the province of Batangas in the region. While tourism is significant in Laguna, most of the visitors to these spots are from foreign countries rather than other provinces in Calabarzon.


Laguna has many historical sites that reflect the diverse periods of history in the province. Some notable examples include the Rizal Shrine in Calamba, which is the birthplace of the Philippines' national hero José Rizal, as well as the Alberto House in Biñan, the Cuartel de Santo Domingo in Santa Rosa, the Pagsanjan Municipal Hall, the Pagsanjan Arch, the Nagcarlan Underground Cemetery, and the Emilio Jacinto Shrine in Santa Cruz. Additionally, there are many historical markers in Laguna, such as the Japanese Garden in Cavinti and the Homma-Yamashita Shrine in Los Baños, which commemorate the Japanese occupation of the Philippines during World War II, as well as memorial sites for the Battle of Sambat in Pagsanjan and the Battle of Mabitac in Mabitac, and monuments like the Bantayog ng Kagitingan, Bantayog ng mga Bayani, Trece Martires Monument, and the Bonifacio Monument in San Pablo.


In addition to its historical sites, Laguna also has a number of museums that offer both historical and educational experiences. These include the Pila Memorial Museum in Pila, the Salvador Laurel Museum and Library in San Pedro, the Museo de Santa Rosa in Santa Rosa, the Danilo Daleňa House of Paintings in Pakil, the Bahay Laguna in Magdalena, and the Monterola Museum in Nagcarlan. Los Baños is also home to several museums such as the IRRI Riceworld Museum, the Museum of Natural History, the Museum of the Wildlife Collections of Dr. Dioscoro Rabor, and the Entomology Museum. Furthermore, Laguna is also home to some of the oldest churches in the Philippines dating back to the late 16th century. The St. James the Apostle Parish Church in Paete and the Saint Gregory the Great Parish Church in Majayjay are considered to be the oldest in the province, dating back to 1578 and 1571 respectively.

There are various historical churches located in different provinces, such as the Saint Paul the First Hermit Cathedral in San Pablo which serves as the seat of the Bishop of the Roman Catholic Diocese of San Pablo, the Transfiguration of our Lord Parish Church in Cavinti, the Nuestra Señora de Candelaria Parish Church in Mabitac, the Immaculate Conception Parish Church in Los Baños, the Saint John the Baptist Church in Liliw, the St. John the Baptist Church in Calamba where Jose Rizal was baptized, the Saint Peter of Alcantara Parish Church in Pakil which houses the Our Lady of Turumba painting, the Immaculate Conception Parish Church in Santa Cruz, St. Polycarp Church and the Diocesan Shrine of San Vicente Ferrer in Cabuyao, Santa Rose de Lima Parish Church in Santa Rosa, and the Diocesan Shrine of Jesus in the Holy Sepulchre (Lolo Uweng) and the San Pedro Apostol Parish Church in San Pedro. The Lolo Uweng Church, also known as the Quiapo Church of the South.


Due to its geography and climate, Laguna boasts a variety of natural tourist destinations. The Laguna Volcanic Field, a collection of 200 volcanoes and maars, is located in the province, with Mount Makiling, considered a sacred mountain, as its highest point. The Seven Lakes of San Pablo are also part of this field. Mount Banahaw, which is shared with the province of Quezon, is the highest peak in Laguna. The province also has several waterfalls, such as Pagsanjan Falls, Buruwisan Falls in Siniloan, Botocan and Taytay Falls in Majayjay, Talon ng Aliw and Hulugan Falls, both in Luisiana. Other popular attractions include the Cavinti Underground River and Caves Complex, the Simbahang Bato in Luisiana, the Dalitiwan River in Majayjay, and the Crocodile Park in Los Baños.

In addition to natural tourist spots, Laguna also offers a variety of man-made attractions and parks. The Enchanted Kingdom theme park, which is recognized by the Department of Tourism, is located in Santa Rosa. Other parks in the province include Valesol Haven, Boy Scouts of the Philippines Camp, Maria Makiling Park and Makiling Botanical Garden, all found in Los Baños, Wawa Park in Paete and the Public Plaza of San Pablo. Calamba and Los Baños are particularly well-known for their resorts and hot springs, earning them the title of "Resort Capital of the Philippines". Other popular sites include the Turumba Swimming Pool in Pakil, Caliraya Lake, Dagatan Boulevard in San Pablo and the Sampaloc Lake Staircase.
