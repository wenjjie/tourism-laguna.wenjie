Province of Laguna

Education

In Laguna, there is a high level of educational attainment. In 2010, a large portion of the population (about 2,382,512 out of 2,669,847) had received some form of education. The literacy rate in 2000 was 96.22%. Many residents of Laguna have completed high school, and a significant number of overseas workers from the area have college degrees. The area is home to 586 public schools, including 458 primary schools and 128 secondary schools. San Pablo has the most public schools with 79, while Rizal has the least with only four.

Laguna is known for its diverse educational institutions, with approximately 18 universities and 37 colleges in the region. There are three state-run universities in Laguna, including Laguna State Polytechnic University, Pamantasan ng Cabuyao, and Polytechnic University of the Philippines, all of which have a total of 11 campuses. The University of the Philippines Los Baños (UPLB), which was established in 1909, is one of the oldest universities in the Philippines. Additionally, the International Rice Research Institute, which is known globally for its rice research and technology advancements, is located on the UPLB campus.

In addition to public universities, Laguna also boasts a number of private colleges. Some of these institutions, such as Lyceum of the Philippines University-Laguna, De La Salle University - Laguna Campus, Colegio San Agustin, San Sebastian College – Recoletos, Colegio de San Juan de Letran, Malayan Colleges Laguna, National University Laguna, and Our Lady of Fatima University – Laguna Campus, have main campuses located in Metro Manila but have also established satellite campuses in Laguna. Recently, the University of Santo Tomas and the University of the East announced plans to establish campuses in Santa Rosa, Laguna.

One of the famous school in Laguna is the The Laguna campus extension of De La Salle University since 2012 and is located adjacent to Laguna Technopark in Biñan, Laguna, Philippines.
