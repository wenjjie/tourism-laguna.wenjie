Geography

Laguna is a province located in the north-central section of the Calabarzon region in Luzon, Philippines. It has an area of 1,917.85 km2 and is situated southeast of Metro Manila and surrounded by other provinces such as Rizal, Quezon, Batangas, and Cavite. It is the third largest province in the Calabarzon region and has 63rd largest in the entire country. The municipalities of Cavinti and the city of San Pablo have the largest land areas, while the municipality of Victoria has the smallest land area. The province is situated on the southern shores of Laguna de Bay, the largest lake in the country, and is home to 24 mountains, including inactive volcanoes. The highest peak in Laguna is Mt. Banahaw, which is an active complex stratovolcano. Laguna is also home to the Laguna Volcanic Field, which is composed of over 200 dormant and monogenetic maars, crater lakes, scoria cones and stratovolcanoes, the tallest of which is Mount Makiling.

The city of San Pablo is well known for its seven crater lakes, the largest of which is Lake Sampaloc. In addition to these lakes, there are also two man-made reservoir lakes, Lake Caliraya and Lumot Lake, which were created by the construction of the Caliraya Dam and the Lumot Dam. Due to its proximity to Laguna de Bay, the province is home to many of its tributaries, one of the most well-known being the Bumbungan River, also known as the Pagsanjan River, which is named after the town of Pagsanjan and its falls, the Pagsanjan Falls, which is a three-drop waterfall with a longest drop of 120 m. Laguna has a significant amount of agricultural land, mostly found in low-lying areas, and a large amount of forest land, situated near Mount Makiling and further south towards Quezon. The province has an estimated 16,205 ha of forest cover, which ranks it third in the region.

Climate

The climate of the province of Laguna varies depending on location. Some parts of the province have a dry season from November to April and a rainy season during the rest of the year, while other areas have a more evenly distributed rainfall throughout the year. The eastern and southernmost portions do not have a distinct dry or wet season. Laguna is classified under the Köppen climate classification as having a Tropical monsoon climate mostly in the areas of Calamba to Calauan. However, the area stretching from Biñan to Cabuyao is classified as having a Tropical savanna climate and the area from Luisiana to Pangil has a tropical rainforest climate.

Demographics

The population of Laguna in the 2020 census was 3,382,193 people, with a population density of 1,800 people per square kilometer. Calamba is the most populous city in Laguna, accounting for 16% of the total population, with 539,671 inhabitants, while Famy is the least populous municipality with a population of 16,791. San Pedro has the highest population density with 14,000 people per square kilometer, while Cavinti has the lowest population density with 120 people per square kilometer. The majority of the population lives in the cities of San Pedro, Biñan, Santa Rosa, Cabuyao, Calamba and San Pablo, which account for 68.85% of the total population of the province. Laguna is the 3rd most populous province in the Philippines and also the 3rd densest. The median age of the population is 24.9 years.

Most of the people in Laguna speak Tagalog, with 99.2% of the population fluent in it. Other languages spoken include Ilocano, Bicolano, and Waray. A significant portion of the population can read and speak English and Filipino. In 2000, the literacy rate in Laguna was 96.22%.

Religion

Laguna has a long history of being one of the first provinces in the Philippines to be visited by Christian missionaries. Today, the majority of the population in the province, like the rest of the country, is Christian with the majority being Roman Catholic. The Diocese of San Pablo is the only Roman Catholic diocese in Laguna. Other Christian denominations present in the province include the Philippine Independent Church, the Iglesia ni Cristo, Baptists, Ang Dating Daan, and other Mainline Protestants. Non-Christian religions practiced in Laguna include Islam, animism, Anitism, or atheism.

Economy

Laguna is a thriving economy with a population of 3,382,193 people, and a total area of 1,760 km2. The province produces a significant amount of coconuts, rice, sugar, citrus fruits, lanzones, and other products. It is also a popular tourist destination, known for its natural beauty spots, including Pagsanjan Falls, Calamba and Los Baños hot springs, Mount Makiling, Caliraya Lake and many others. The level of development varies across the province, with towns near Metro Manila being more industrialized, while inner towns continue to engage in agricultural production and agri-based industries and cottage and small-scale industries. In 2015, Laguna reported an equity of PHP 4.474 billion, which is the second highest in the region behind Cavite.

Laguna is known as the "Detroit of the Philippines" due to the presence of major vehicle manufacturers in the city of Santa Rosa. It is also considered as the "Silicon Valley of the Philippines" because of the many electronic and semi-conductor companies operating in the province. Additionally, Laguna is also referred to as the "Resort Capital of the Philippines" as it has over 700 hot spring resorts in the areas of Calamba and Los Baños.

Industry

Laguna is home to 21 economic zones that are registered under the Philippine Economic Zone Authority. The majority of these economic zones are located in Santa Rosa, Biñan, and Calamba. The largest economic zone in Laguna is the Laguna Technopark, which is located in Santa Rosa and Biñan and has an area of 337.215 hectares. The province is also home to a large automotive industry, with companies such as Toyota Motor Philippines, Nissan Motor Philippines, Honda Philippines Incorporated, and Mitsubishi Motors Philippines all having plants in Santa Rosa.

Laguna has a growing business process outsourcing industry, with companies such as IBM and Convergys establishing offices in Santa Rosa. The PEZA lists 4 IT parks and 1 IT Center in Laguna. The province also continues to see an increasing number of business districts and shopping malls, which bring opportunities and attractive places for dining and relaxation.
